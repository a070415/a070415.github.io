How to Use

This calculator helps you measure how your commute time, work intensity, and free time impact your overall happiness level. Adjust each slider to reflect your actual daily routine, then click “Calculate Score.” The result gives a numerical score representing your estimated commute satisfaction and lifestyle balance.

The goal is to visualize how small lifestyle changes can lead to better emotional well-being. Even slight adjustments—like cutting down your commute by ten minutes, walking instead of driving once a week, or scheduling daily free time—can significantly boost happiness and productivity. This simple model encourages reflection on how you spend your time every day.

Tips for Improving Your Score

If your score is low, start by observing which factor influences it most. Long commutes often lead to fatigue and frustration, while low free time can cause stress and imbalance. Try optimizing your commute hours or incorporating flexible work options when possible. If your workload is consistently high, remember that taking short breaks can restore focus and creativity.

Physical activity and sunlight exposure are strongly linked to mental well-being. If walking or cycling is feasible, consider trying it a few times per week. On public transport, turn your travel into quiet time—listen to music, audiobooks, or simply rest your mind. The key idea is not to eliminate stress completely but to design a daily rhythm that feels sustainable and enjoyable.

By tracking your score regularly, you’ll begin to recognize patterns that affect your happiness. Over time, this awareness can lead to long-term improvements in work-life balance, health, and emotional stability.
